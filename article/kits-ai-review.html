Kits AI Review: I Cloned My Voice and Made It Sing. The Results Were Uneven.

By Alex · Updated June 4, 2026 · AI Pickz

How we tested: Hands-on testing over multiple days. Paid plans unless noted. Full methodology on our About page.

Disclosure: Some links are affiliate links. We may earn a commission at no extra cost to you.

June 4, 2026 6 min read Voice & Audio

I didn't think AI could make me sound like a decent singer. My voice is best described as "enthusiastic." Kits AI claimed it could take my speaking voice, clone it, and have it sing in any style.

The short version: it went further than I expected. But not as far as the marketing suggests.

The one feature that sets it apart

Most AI voice tools do text-to-speech. Kits AI does something different: voice-to-voice singing conversion. Upload a recording of yourself speaking or humming, and it transforms that audio into sung vocals. Same melody as a reference track, but with your voice.

This isn't cloning a celebrity voice and having it sing. That's a separate feature. The singing conversion takes your voice and makes it carry a tune.

Priya, a music producer in Los Angeles, tried it for a client pitch. "I needed a male vocal for a 30-second ad spot. I did not have a male singer on call. I fed it a voice sample recorded in falsetto and let Kits do the rest. The client could not tell." She still keeps session singers for longer projects, but for quick drafts Kits saved her a booking fee.

What I tested

I recorded a 60-second sample of me speaking normally. Kits AI processed it through instant cloning in about 2 minutes. Then I uploaded an instrumental track and a reference melody. The voice-to-voice converter mapped my cloned voice onto the melody.

First attempt: a slow folk song. The result was clean. Pitch tracking held up. My cloned voice sounded like I was trying to sing, which is what I wanted. The consonants were crisp. Breath control better than mine.

Second attempt: a faster pop track with a wider range. This is where it broke. High notes sounded thin. Artificial vibrato came out as a warble. Timing slipped on fast syllables. Push the pitch correction too far and the voice turned metallic.

Jake, a YouTube creator in Austin who makes parody song videos, had a similar experience. "For a simple melody, it works great. I did a Johnny Cash style cover and it fooled my subscribers for about 30 seconds. But when I tried something with runs and vocal fry, it turned into a robot having a seizure."

The other stuff it does

Kits also includes a vocal remover, stem splitter, text-to-speech, and a voice designer that blends two voices. The vocal remover works on simple arrangements. On dense mixes, stems get muddy. The voice designer is interesting. I blended my voice with a preset pop vocal. The result was pleasant but not remarkable.

Pricing and who it is for

Free: 15 conversion minutes, no custom voice slots. Enough for a few experiments. Starter at $10/month adds unlimited conversions, 2 voice slots, and download capability. Producer at $30/month unlocks unlimited voice slots and high-fidelity cloning. Professional at $60/month removes all limits.

You need at least Starter to export your work. Producer for sound quality that approaches usable.

What is still not great

Latency. Processing a voice clone takes 2-3 minutes. Converting a song takes another 2-4. Iterating on a mix means constant pauses. Kits is a studio tool, not a live processor.

Language support is patchy. English works well. Other languages exist but accent rendering is inconsistent. Spanish lyrics came out with flat consonants in my tests.

Download minutes cap is frustrating. 15 minutes per month on Starter means counting every export.

The verdict

Buy if

You make quick demo vocals, need a placeholder singer for a mix, or want to experiment with your own voice in genres you cannot naturally sing. The singing conversion feature is useful for rapid prototyping.

Skip if

You need polished, release ready vocals. The artifacts on complex melodies are still too obvious. Hire a session singer. Also skip if you want live processing. Kits works in batches, not real time.

Kits AI does one thing few tools attempt: turning a speaking voice into a singing voice. It works best on simple, slow melodies with narrow ranges. Push it into fast pop or wide vibrato, and the seams show. For a $10 experiment it's worth trying. For polished vocals, hire a singer.
