Can 4 AI Tools Save You Time? I Tested Them for a Week to Find Out.

By Alex · Updated June 5, 2026 · AI Pickz

How we tested: Hands-on testing over multiple days. Paid plans unless noted. Full methodology on our About page.

Disclosure: Some links are affiliate links. We may earn a commission at no extra cost to you.

June 5, 2026 6 min read Weekly Picks

About 80% of people who try a new AI tool stop opening it after the first week. I wanted to understand why. So I picked four tools I had never used and forced myself to use each one for real work, no skipping days.

The rule was simple. If a tool did not save more time than it cost to set up, it was gone. If it solved something I could not already do, it stayed. No second chances for tools that wasted my time on day one.

Clockwise: out by day three

Clockwise claims it can optimize your calendar so you stop losing hours to meeting juggling. That sounds great if you, like me, spend mornings moving calls around instead of working.

Setup took thirty minutes. Connect Google Calendar, mark preferences, block focus time, tell it which meetings are movable. I did all of it.

The first thing it did was reschedule a 1:1 into a slot I had marked as unavailable. I spent more time fixing its changes than I saved. By day three, I uninstalled it. The idea is good. The execution is not there yet.

Lex.page: stayed but limited

Lex.page is the opposite of Clockwise. Zero setup. Open the page and start typing. A clean, distraction-free editor with good typography and a minimal interface.

I wrote two draft posts in it this week. The writing experience is better than Google Docs. No toolbar clutter, no formatting distractions. Just the cursor and the text.

But that is all it does. There is no collaboration. No comments. No track changes. I export everything to Google Docs for editing. Lex is a nice first-draft tool. It is not a full writing platform.

Worth a bookmark. Not worth a subscription.

ElevenLabs: impressive until the price hits

I needed a voiceover for a short video. ElevenLabs felt like overkill for thirty seconds of audio. But I tried it anyway.

The voice library is enormous. Hundreds of options sorted by style, gender, and accent. I picked one, typed my script, and had audio in about thirty seconds. The pacing was stiff on the first try. I tweaked the stability slider and regenerated. Better. By the third attempt, it sounded real.

Then I checked the pricing. The Creator plan is $22 a month. The $99 Pro plan unlocks the best voices. For a single short voiceover, that is hard to justify.

Rachel, a freelance video editor in Austin I talked to, uses ElevenLabs for client projects. She told me "the quality is the best I have found, but I only pay for it when I have work lined up. I cancel between projects." That is probably the smartest way to use it.

Meta AI: the one that stayed

Meta AI surprised me the most. I manage a few brand accounts and keeping up with comment replies is tedious. Meta AI can draft responses based on your brand voice.

I set it up in under a minute. No configuration. No training data. It scanned my previous replies and generated suggestions that matched my tone. When someone asked about pricing, it drafted three options. One was polite. One was direct. One added a personal touch I would not have thought of.

Tom, a community manager in Chicago who runs social for a SaaS company, told me Meta AI "saved me about an hour a day" on engagement. I see why. It solves a real pain point with zero friction. No setup, no learning curve, no subscription upsell.

What survived the week

Worth keeping:

Meta AI. It solves something broken without asking you to change your workflow. That is rare.

Worth trying (with caveats):

Lex.page for first drafts. ElevenLabs for short projects if you cancel between months.

Not there yet:

Clockwise. The concept is appealing. The execution needs work.

The lesson from this week is simple. Most AI tools are solutions looking for a problem. The ones that survive are the ones that fix something you already know is broken without making you jump through hoops.

Meta AI got that right. The others either overpromised or solved a problem I did not have. Next week, I am testing a different batch. If the pattern holds, maybe one more will earn a permanent spot.
