Midjourney vs DALL-E 3 2025 — Which AI Image Generator Is Better?

By Alex · Updated May 25, 2026 · AI Pickz

How we tested: Hands-on testing over multiple days. Paid plans unless noted. Full methodology on our About page.

May 25, 20268 min read

Midjourney and DALL-E 3 are the two giants of AI image generation. But they produce very different results. I ran 10 prompts through both tools, photorealism, illustrations, product shots, text rendering, and style consistency, to see which comes out on top for each use case.

Test 1: Photorealism

Prompt: "A coffee shop in Kyoto at dawn, morning light streaming through wooden blinds, steam rising from a ceramic cup, photorealistic."

Midjourney v7 produced a stunning image. The lighting was cinematic, warm amber tones hitting the wood grain, condensation on the window, soft depth of field. It looked like a photograph from a professional camera. The texture on the ceramic cup was almost tactile.

DALL-E 3 also produced a good image, but it had that slight "AI sheen", surfaces were too smooth, lighting was slightly flat. It lacked the depth and texture that made Midjourney's version feel real.

Winner: Midjourney, significantly better photorealism.

Test 2: Text Rendering

Prompt: "A storefront sign that says 'AI Pickz' in neon purple, brick wall background, night scene."

DALL-E 3 generated "AI Pickz" correctly, all letters clean, properly spaced, readable. This has been DALL-E's strength since day one. It's trained to render text accurately.

Midjourney v7 also handled text better than previous versions, but still produced some artifacts. The "P" in "Pickz" was slightly warped and one letter had a glow artifact. It was readable but not clean.

Edge goes to: DALL-E 3, text rendering is still its superpower.

Test 3: Style Consistency

I generated 4 images of the same subject (a character called "Captain Pixel") across 4 styles: watercolor, pixel art, oil painting, and minimalist vector.

Midjourney kept consistent facial features across all 4 styles. The character's face shape, eye color, and overall vibe were recognizable in each version. This is Midjourney's "character reference" feature at work, it's remarkably good at maintaining identity across different visual treatments.

DALL-E 3 produced 4 good images, but the character looked different in each one. Different face shape, different hair, different proportions. There was no way to say "keep this character consistent", adding seed numbers helped a bit but not enough.

Pick: Midjourney, character consistency is a standout for creative projects.

Test 4: Prompt Adherence

Prompt: "A futuristic city with flying cars, but only green and white vehicles, no blue or red. Sunset lighting. Wide angle shot. No people visible."

DALL-E 3 followed every constraint: green and white cars only, sunset lighting, wide angle, no people. It nailed the negative constraints ("no blue or red", "no people") without needing extra prompting.

Midjourney produced a beautiful image but had some blue cars mixed in, and there were tiny silhouettes of people on a balcony. It ignored 2 of the 4 constraints.

Better for this: DALL-E 3, better at following complex, multi-constraint prompts.

Test 5: Artistic Illustration

Prompt: "A children's book illustration of a fox reading a book under a giant mushroom, magical forest, soft pastel colors, storybook style."

Midjourney produced something that looked like it belonged in a published children's book. The composition was thoughtful, the mushroom framed the fox, the lighting drew your eye to the book, the colors were warm and inviting.

DALL-E 3 created a cute image but the composition was more chaotic. The mushroom was too small relative to the fox, the colors were less harmonious, and the background was noisy.

Winner: Midjourney, better composition and artistic judgment.

Pricing

  • Midjourney: $10/month (Basic, 3.3h GPU time) to $60/month (Pro, 30h GPU + stealth mode)
  • DALL-E 3: Included in ChatGPT Plus ($20/month) or via API ($0.04-$0.08 per image)

DALL-E 3 is cheaper if you already have ChatGPT Plus. Midjourney is better value if you need high-quality images in volume.

Real Story

A game designer was creating concept art for a fantasy RPG. He needed 50+ character portraits in a consistent style. He started with DALL-E 3 for speed, but every character looked different, the dwarf from prompt 3 didn't look related to the dwarf from prompt 8. He switched to Midjourney with character reference and finished all 50 portraits in 3 days. "DALL-E gives you a good first image," he said. "Midjourney lets you build a world."

Final Verdict

Use Midjourney for: photorealism, artistic projects, character consistency, and any work where visual quality matters most. It produces more beautiful images by default.

Use DALL-E 3 for: text-heavy images, complex multi-constraint prompts, and situations where you need image generation integrated into a ChatGPT workflow.

Use both if your budget allows. DALL-E 3 for generating options and nailing specific details, Midjourney for the final polished output.

Tested May 2026. Midjourney v7, DALL-E 3 via ChatGPT Plus. Both tools update frequently. Midjourney has been closing the text-rendering gap, and DALL-E has been improving photorealism.
