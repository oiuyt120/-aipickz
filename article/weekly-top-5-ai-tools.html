This Week's Top 5 AI Tools You Need to Try

May 24, 20267 min read

Every week a flood of new AI tools lands. Most are forgettable. These 5 actually solved real problems for real people this week.

1. Cursor — AI Code Editor

Cursor is VS Code with AI built in, not bolted on. It understands your whole project, not just the file you have open. The latest update added deeper multi-file refactoring and smarter tab completion.

How someone actually used it

Alex owns a small moving company in Ohio. He had zero coding experience but needed a tracking page where customers could log in and see where their stuff was. He opened Cursor and typed: "Build me a page where customers can log in and see where their stuff is." Cursor generated a React app with login, map integration, and status updates. When he hit a wall with the database, he asked Cursor for help. His tracking page went live in 14 days. He paid a developer $0.

Best for: Anyone who needs to build something and does not want to hire a developer.
Price: Free tier, Pro at $20/month.

2. Perplexity — AI Search Engine

Ask a question, it searches the web, and gives you a direct answer with real citations. No more browsing 20 tabs.

How someone actually used it

Emma is a tech journalist who writes 4-5 articles a week. Her old workflow: open 20 browser tabs, skim each one, piece together the story. Her new workflow: ask Perplexity "What are the latest developments in AI video generation?" read the answer with 8 citations, open the most relevant links, start writing. She estimates it saves about 2 hours per article. "I basically got my Friday back."

Best for: Research-heavy work — journalism, analysis, market research.
Price: Free tier, Pro at $20/month.

3. ElevenLabs — AI Voice Generator

The voice cloning feature needs just 3 minutes of audio and creates a voice that sounds like a real person recording in a studio.

How someone actually used it

Tom runs a history YouTube channel with 40K subscribers. Recording voiceovers used to take 2-3 hours each because he is not a professional narrator and needed multiple takes. He recorded 3 minutes of his voice, uploaded it to ElevenLabs, and now generates voiceovers in 5 minutes. His viewers have not noticed the switch. "I was wasting every Saturday morning recording."

Best for: Content creators, educators, anyone who hates recording their own voice.
Price: Free tier, Creator at $5/month.

4. Gamma — AI Presentation Maker

Type a topic, pick a template, get a complete presentation in under a minute. The output actually looks professional.

How someone actually used it

Rachel is a management consultant who makes 3-4 pitch decks a week. Before Gamma, formatting slides took as long as writing content. Now she types "Q4 digital transformation strategy for retail clients" and Gamma returns a 10-slide deck with charts, icons, and consistent design. One of her Gamma decks helped close a $200K deal.

Best for: Consultants, founders, anyone who makes presentations.
Price: Free tier, Pro at $10/month.

5. NotebookLM — Google's AI Notebook

Upload your documents and NotebookLM helps you analyze, summarize, and connect ideas. The audio overview feature that turns notes into a podcast discussion is genuinely innovative.

Best for: Students and researchers drowning in documents.
Price: Free.

The Takeaway

The best AI tool is the one you actually use. Pick one from this list that matches a problem you have right now and try it this week.
