Grok Review: I Used xAI's Chatbot for a Week — Day 1 vs Day 7

By Alex · Updated June 3, 2026 · AI Pickz

How we tested: Standard plan tested of Grok over multiple days. Full methodology on my About page.

June 3, 2026 · 6 min read

I didn't think I'd learn anything new from another AI chatbot. Seven tools tested this year alone. ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Perplexity, Pi, Poe, Character.AI — what could Grok possibly do that they hadn't already done?

I was wrong. Not about everything. But wrong enough that the week changed how I think about what an AI assistant should be.

Day 1: The Setup

Getting started with Grok takes about ten seconds. If you already pay for X Premium, it lives in the sidebar of X.com and the mobile app. No separate login. No new subscription. Premium+ unlocks the full Grok: faster responses, file uploads, and something called DeepSearch.

My first query was simple: "Summarize the AI news from this week." Grok pulled from live X posts, links shared by people I follow, and ongoing discussions. It wasn't listing headlines. It was showing me what people in the space were actually arguing about.

That caught me off guard. ChatGPT pulls from training data. Perplexity searches the web. Grok searches X — the conversations that happen before articles get written.

Day 1 verdict: impressive for real time stuff. Weird for everything else.

Day 4: Hitting the Edges

By midweek I'd found a rhythm. Grok for news and trends. ChatGPT for deep work.

The X integration is useful in unexpected ways. I asked it to analyze engagement patterns on my own tweets. It broke down which topics drove replies versus likes. It spotted something I'd missed: my Tuesday morning posts consistently underperformed, not because the content was worse, but because my audience showed up later in the day.

But here's where it got frustrating. DeepSearch is powerful but inconsistent. One query returns a detailed report with citations. The next gives you a confident-sounding paragraph with nothing to back it up. When I pressed for sources, they were sometimes just wrong.

Maria, a social media manager in Chicago, told me she uses Grok specifically for competitor research on X. "I asked it to analyze 500 posts from three competitors. It took two minutes. The format was messy, but the insights would've taken me four hours to find manually."

Speed over reliability. You move fast, you verify later.

Day 7: What Stuck

After a week, Grok earned a spot in my workflow. A narrow one.

I kept it for:

  • Real time research: what's happening on X right now
  • Profile analysis: who engages with whose content and how
  • News summarization with social context, not just headlines

I did not keep it for:

  • Writing: it's okay, but Claude is better
  • Coding: it can, but why would you
  • Deep analysis: DeepSearch is too inconsistent for anything that matters

Carlos, a product manager in Berlin, described the role best. "Grok is my gossip friend. ChatGPT is my research assistant. Claude is my editor. They don't compete. They sit at different tables in the same cafeteria."

The Verdict

Grok is the best chatbot for one specific thing: understanding what's happening on X in real time. If that matters to you, it's worth keeping open. If you don't use X, you probably don't need it.

But: ChatGPT wins for general knowledge. Claude wins for writing. Perplexity wins for structured research. Grok wins for pulse. And sometimes pulse is exactly what you need.
