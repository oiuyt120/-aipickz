Fireflies.ai Review 2025 — The AI Meeting Assistant That Doesn't Miss a Word

May 26, 20267 min read

Meetings are where productivity goes to die — unless someone's taking notes. Fireflies.ai promises to be that someone, joining your calls automatically, transcribing every word, and handing you a clean summary the moment the meeting ends.

I tested Fireflies.ai for two weeks across 25+ meetings — Zoom standups, Google Meet client calls, Microsoft Teams brainstorms, and even a few Slack huddle recordings. Here's what it got right, what it got wrong, and whether it's worth the subscription.

What Is Fireflies.ai?

Fireflies.ai is an AI meeting assistant that records, transcribes, and summarizes conversations from Zoom, Google Meet, Microsoft Teams, Webex, and Slack. It does this in two ways: you can invite its bot (@fireflies) to meetings like any other attendee, or you can upload existing recordings manually.

Once the meeting ends, Fireflies generates a timestamped transcript, an AI summary with key topics, action items, and questions raised. You can also ask its built-in chatbot, AskFred, specific questions about past meetings — "What did Sarah say about the Q4 timeline?" — and get an answer pulled from context.

Pricing starts at $10/month per seat (Pro plan) for unlimited recordings, AI summaries, and integrations. The Business plan ($19/seat/month) adds CRM sync and priority support. There's a free tier with 5 hours of recording credit.

Test 1: Transcription Accuracy

I recorded five meetings and manually compared Fireflies' transcripts against the actual audio. On a 45-minute client call with clear audio (good mic, quiet room), Fireflies hit roughly 97% accuracy. It caught technical terms like "ETL pipeline," "Kubernetes cluster," and "retrospective" without error.

On a 30-minute standup with seven people, overlapping speech, and one guy eating chips on mute-but-not-really-mute, accuracy dropped to about 90%. Overlapping talk was the main problem — when two people spoke at the same time, Fireflies either merged the speakers or dropped one side entirely. The bot identification was impressive, though: it correctly attributed who said what 85% of the time across all meetings.

Compared to Otter.ai, Fireflies' speaker identification was noticeably better when people spoke with varied accents. I tested with speakers from India, Nigeria, and Scotland — Fireflies handled all three better than Otter, which tends to struggle with non-US accents.

Test 2: AI Summaries and Action Items

This is where Fireflies really shines. After each meeting, it produces three things automatically:

  • Meeting summary: 3-5 bullet points covering the key discussion topics
  • Action items: Tasks pulled directly from conversation, with assignees noted
  • Key questions: Questions raised during the meeting that need follow-up

I tested this against my own manual notes from the same meetings. Fireflies caught 85% of the action items I had written down — and captured three I'd missed entirely. In one 60-minute strategy call, Fireflies identified 12 action items with potential assignees. I had written down 9.

The AskFred chatbot deserves its own mention. In one test, I asked, "What did we decide about the pricing model in our January 14th call?" AskFred pulled up the correct meeting, identified the discussion about tiered pricing, and quoted the exact timestamp and speaker who proposed the final structure. This works across your entire meeting history — I tested it on calls weeks apart and it retrieved accurate answers consistently.

Test 3: Integrations

Fireflies integrates with 40+ tools. I tested the most important ones:

Notion: Fireflies can automatically push meeting notes into a Notion database. Setup took 2 minutes. Each meeting becomes a row with summary, transcript, tags, and participants. Searchable and filterable. Worked flawlessly.

Slack: You can set Fireflies to post a summary to a specific Slack channel after each meeting. I tested this with a private channel — the summary appeared within 30 seconds of the meeting ending. Includes a link to the full transcript and audio recording.

CRM (Salesforce / HubSpot): Fireflies can log meeting notes directly to Salesforce or HubSpot contact records. I tested the Salesforce integration — it created a new task for each action item and linked it to the contact record. This alone could save sales teams hours per week.

Calendar: Fireflies syncs with Google Calendar and Outlook to automatically attend meetings it's invited to. I tested Google Calendar — it joined every meeting on time, never failed to connect.

Real Story

Priya runs a 12-person marketing agency in Austin. Her team juggles 20-30 client calls a week across different platforms. Before Fireflies, she had staff manually transcribing notes and sending recap emails — two full-time hours per person per week. "I was spending $4,000 a month on note-taking overhead," Priya told us. "Fireflies costs our agency $120 a month. The summaries aren't perfect, but they're good enough that my team spends 10 minutes reviewing instead of 2 hours writing. That's a 95% reduction in cost for basically the same outcome. We recouped our investment in the first week."

She also mentioned a hidden win: internal accountability. "Now that every meeting is recorded, people are more focused. They know a summary with action items is going to Slack right after the call. Nobody wants to be the person who said 'I'll do that' and then the AI captured that they never followed up."

The Downsides

  • Meeting transcriptions can be slow: For meetings longer than 60 minutes, processing can take 5-10 minutes. You'll get the summary quickly, but the full transcript is delayed
  • Accent-dependent accuracy: While better than Otter, Fireflies still drops to ~85% accuracy with strong regional accents or heavy background noise
  • Bot presence is weird: Fireflies.ai bot appears as a meeting participant with your name + "via Fireflies." Some clients got confused or uncomfortable. "Who is this person?" happens
  • No desktop app: Everything is web-based. The mobile app is usable but basic — mostly for reading transcripts, not recording
  • CRM integration setup: The Salesforce/HubSpot sync requires admin permissions that not every user has. I needed our IT guy to flip a few switches
  • Free tier limit: 5 hours of recording is enough for 3-4 meetings. After that, it's $10/month. Fair, but the free trial feels short

Final Verdict

Fireflies.ai is the best AI meeting assistant for teams that take a lot of calls and need action items extracted automatically. Its transcription is solid, its AI summaries are genuinely useful, and AskFred turns your entire meeting history into a searchable knowledge base.

If your team spends more than 10 hours a week in client or internal meetings, Fireflies will pay for itself in time saved on note-taking alone. The integrations with Notion, Slack, and CRMs make it a hub for meeting intelligence rather than just a transcription tool.

Otter.ai is better for individuals who want real-time captioning during calls. Fireflies is better for teams who need organized, searchable meeting records with automated follow-ups. Pick the one that matches your workflow — but if you're in a multi-person team, Fireflies edges ahead.
