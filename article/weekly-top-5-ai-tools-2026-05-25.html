This Week's Top 5 AI Tools — May 25, 2026

May 25, 20265 min read

Every week we test new AI tools and pick the ones worth your time. This week: an emotional AI voice that actually works, a Chinese model that costs next to nothing, and image generation that keeps getting better.

1. Hume AI — Emotional Voice Synthesis

What it is: An AI voice generator that analyzes the emotional content of your text and adjusts tone, pacing, and emphasis accordingly.

Why it made the list: Most AI voice tools read text. Hume AI performs it. For podcasters, audiobook creators, and content producers, this is the first AI voice that doesn't sound like a robot trying to sound human — it sounds like a human who's actually paying attention to what they're saying.

Best for: Narrative content, podcast ad reads, character voices in audiobooks

Price: $0.10/min (API)

2. DeepSeek V3 — The $0.48 Model

What it is: A Chinese Mixture-of-Experts model that matches GPT-4o on coding and reasoning at 5% of the price.

Why it made the list: If you're paying API bills out of pocket, DeepSeek changes the math. A startup processing 10 million tokens daily would pay $4.80 instead of $100. The quality gap is real but narrow — about 90% of GPT-4o for most tasks.

Best for: High-volume coding, translation (especially Chinese-English), budget-constrained projects

Price: $0.48/M input tokens, $1.96/M output

3. Midjourney v7 — Best Photorealism Gets Better

What it is: The latest version of Midjourney, with improved text rendering, better style consistency, and a new character reference feature.

Why it made the list: Midjourney v7 fixed two of its biggest weaknesses — text rendering (still not DALL-E level, but close) and character consistency (now genuinely usable for projects that need the same character across multiple images). The photorealism is still best-in-class.

Best for: Photorealism, artistic projects, character-consistent image series

Price: $10-60/month

4. Grammarly — Still the Best Editing Tool

What it is: The AI writing assistant that focuses on improving your writing rather than replacing you.

Why it made the list: In a world obsessed with AI generation, Grammarly's editing-first approach feels refreshingly practical. The 2025 updates added better tone detection, smarter conciseness suggestions, and a "clarity score" that genuinely helps you write better. At $12/month, it's the cheapest tool on this list and the most universally useful.

Best for: Anyone who writes emails, documents, or messages in English

Price: $12/month (Premium)

5. GitHub Copilot — Finally Competitive With Cursor

What it is: Microsoft's AI coding assistant with a major 2025 update adding agent mode, multi-file editing, and Copilot Workspace.

Why it made the list: For most of 2024 and early 2025, Cursor was the clear winner in AI-assisted coding. Copilot's 2025 update changed that. Its autocomplete is now faster than Cursor, the new agent mode handles multi-step tasks well, and at $10/month it's half the price. Cursor still wins for complex refactoring, but Copilot is the better value for everyday coding.

Best for: VS Code users who want affordable AI assistance

Price: $10/month (Pro)

Honorable Mention: Notion AI

Notion AI keeps getting better at document generation, summarization, and Q&A within your workspace. If you're already a Notion user, it's worth the $10/month add-on — especially for the "Ask AI" feature that searches across all your documents.

All tools tested the week of May 18-25, 2026. Prices and features may change.
