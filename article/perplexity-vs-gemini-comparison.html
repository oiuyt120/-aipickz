I Used Perplexity for a Week, Then Gemini for a Week — Only One Stayed Open on My Desktop

By Alex · Updated May 31, 2026 · AI Pickz

How we tested: Hands-on testing over multiple days. Paid plans unless noted. Full methodology on our About page.

Disclosure: Some links are affiliate links. We may earn a commission at no extra cost to you.

258| 259|

I had 27 tabs open last Thursday. Research for a client project: competitor analysis, pricing data, technical specs. Six different Google searches, three Reddit threads, two docs I'd already read but couldn't find again.

260| 261|

That's when I decided to try something different.

262| 263|

I'd been hearing about Perplexity and Gemini as "Google killers" for months. Both claim to replace search with AI answers backed by citations. Both have free tiers. Both have Pro plans at $20/month.

264| 265|

So I ran a test. Week one: all my research goes through Perplexity Pro. Week two: everything through Gemini Advanced. No Google. No tab-hopping between ten search results.

266| 267|

Week 1 with Perplexity

268| 269|

Day one felt weird. Instead of typing a query and scanning ten blue links, I typed "competitor pricing analysis for AI note-taking apps" and got a paragraph with footnotes. Each claim linked to a source.

270| 271|

I checked the first three citations. They were real. The fourth was a blog that sort-of supported the claim but was stretching it. Not bad for a first impression.

272| 273|

By day three, the habit had formed. I wasn't missing Google. The collections feature, saved threads grouped by project, became my replacement for bookmarks. I had one for "competitor research," one for "API pricing," one for "investor landscape."

274| 275|

Rachel, a product manager at a health-tech startup in San Francisco, uses Perplexity the same way. "I keep a collection for each feature we're exploring," she told me. "It's faster than my Notion wiki and always has fresh sources."

276| 277|

But by day five, cracks showed. For a niche technical question like "how does LangGraph's checkpointing differ from LangChain's?" Perplexity hallucinated confidently. It cited a GitHub issue that did not say what it claimed. When I asked for clarification, it doubled down.

278| 279|

Week 2 with Gemini

280| 281|

Switching to Gemini Advanced felt like trading a scalpel for a Swiss Army knife.

282| 283|

Gemini's 1M token context window is the headline feature. Upload a PDF, a spreadsheet, a codebase, and it remembers everything. On day one, I dropped in a 47-page competitor report and asked it to extract all pricing tiers by feature. It built a table. That was impressive.

284| 285|

But for quick research, the kind of thing Perplexity handles in 15 seconds, Gemini was slower. It doesn't have Perplexity's citation-forward approach. You get an answer, then you have to dig for sources. Sometimes the sources aren't there at all.

286| 287|

David, a freelance tech writer in Berlin, tried both too. "Perplexity is better when I need to verify facts fast," he said. "Gemini is better when I need to synthesize a whole document. I ended up keeping both."

288| 289|

Day six with Gemini: I hit the message cap on the Advanced tier. Five hours left in my workday. No more long-context queries.

290| 291|

What survived

292| 293|

After two weeks, here's what's still open on my desktop.

294| 295|

Perplexity Pro for daily research. Quick answers with citations I can mostly trust. Collections for project tracking. But I don't use it for deep document analysis or creative exploration.

296| 297|

Gemini Advanced for heavy lifting. Long documents, data extraction, code understanding. But not for quick searches: the lack of source-first answers drives me crazy.

298| 299|
300|

Honest verdict

301|

Edge goes to: Perplexity Pro, because it changes how you search, which matters more for daily work. But Gemini Advanced wins for anyone who regularly processes long documents and needs data extraction. If you do both, you might end up keeping both.

302|
303| 304|

The honest answer? Neither replaces the other. If I had to pick one, I'd keep Perplexity for the work I do most (research), and pull up Gemini when the PDFs pile up.

305| 306|

But that's a cop-out. The harder question is which one is worth $20/month if you can only afford one.

307| 308|

For most people: Perplexity Pro. It changes how you search. Gemini Advanced changes how you process, but you need to already have the documents.

315|

A: Perplexity is better for quick research because it provides citations with every answer. Gemini is slower for this use case but better at processing long documents and extracting structured data from PDFs, spreadsheets, and codebases.
