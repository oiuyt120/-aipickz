I Sold AI-Generated Notion Templates on Gumroad for a Month. Only One Design Actually Worked.

By Alex · Updated June 4, 2026 · AI Pickz

How we tested: Personal experiment: built and sold digital templates over 4 weeks. Full methodology on my About page.

Disclosure: Some links are affiliate links. We may earn a commission at no extra cost to you.

Five templates. A month of data. Real sales numbers. And one big surprise about when AI helps you build something people want to buy.

Three weeks ago I had a Notion dashboard with seventeen views, four databases, and zero customers.

I'd spent three days building it. A content calendar linked to a project tracker linked to a habit tracker. Relations, rollups, formulas. It was complicated. It was impressive. Nobody paid $19 for it.

The template sat on Gumroad for two weeks. Zero sales.

That's when I showed it to Tom, a freelance writer in Austin who buys a lot of Notion templates. His reaction stopped me cold: "This looks like it does everything. I don't need everything. I need a way to track my pitches."

He didn't need a system. He needed one small thing done well.

Using AI to research, not to build

I changed my approach. Instead of asking AI to design a template, I asked it to find out what people wanted. I prompted: "What are the most requested Notion template types on Reddit and Twitter in 2026?"

The answers clustered around simple, single-purpose tools. A reading tracker. A meal planner. A weekly goal board. Nothing with more than two databases. Nothing that needed a tutorial video to understand.

So I built a weekly planner in 40 minutes. One database. Five views. AI wrote the setup guide and the Gumroad description. I priced it at $7.

Before listing it, I showed it to two people. Priya, a product manager in Bangalore, said she'd pay $7. James, a backend engineer in Berlin, said it looked clean but he wouldn't buy it because he uses Google Calendar for scheduling.

That feedback was more useful than any AI-generated feature list. I knew my buyer: not someone who wants to replace their calendar. Someone who already uses Notion and wants one focused tool, not a second job learning a template.

The one that sold

The weekly planner sold three copies in its first seven days. $21 total.

Not life-changing. But it was the first time someone paid me for something AI helped me make. That felt different from posting on social media and getting likes.

Two buyers left notes. One asked for a dark mode version. One said they wanted a section for weekly goals.

I made both changes. The dark mode took 20 minutes with AI writing the CSS. I pushed it as a free update. The goal section took even less time. Neither original buyer purchased again. But two new people bought the updated version the next week.

That pattern repeated: update the template, get a small spike in sales, then nothing until the next update. The templates weren't earning while I slept. They earned when I worked on them.

The one that worked

After a month and six templates, my bestseller is a $5 reading list.

Three fields: book title, author, notes. A checkbox filter for "read" vs "to read." A cover image. That's it. AI wrote the description and the setup instructions. The design took ten minutes.

It has outsold every other template combined.

Elena, a grad student in Chicago who bought it, told me: "I tried an expensive content calendar template. I spent more time learning it than using it. Yours just works."

That's the feedback that matters more than any feature list. "Yours just works." I stared at that sentence for a while before moving on.

One thing I didn't expect: the Gumroad discover page drove zero traffic. Every sale came from a Reddit post or a Twitter thread. I spent more time writing posts about the templates than building the next one.

The honest numbers

Total revenue: $72 from five sales across a month.

Time spent building: about 12 hours across all six templates. Time spent on marketing, engaging in Notion communities, and answering questions: probably double that.

Net hourly rate: roughly $2 an hour. Worse than minimum wage.

I'm not writing this to discourage you. I'm writing it because every "make money with AI" post makes it sound like you build once and the money rolls in. It doesn't work that way. The templates that sold needed constant small updates. The one that took 10 minutes to build needed the most updates because people used it and had opinions.

What I'd do differently

If I started over, I'd skip the complex templates entirely. I'd build five simple ones before touching anything with more than two databases. I'd spend more time in Notion communities watching what people ask for and less time asking AI what to build. I'd price at $5, not $19.

The AI helped with the easy parts: writing copy, formatting tables, generating CSS for dark mode. The hard part — figuring out what someone would pay for — was still entirely on me.

Buy if: you already use Notion and want to see if template creation with AI works for you. It does, for simple things. The AI saves you time on the build, but you still have to figure out what to build.

Skip if: you're looking for passive income. These templates don't earn while you sleep. They earn when you update them, respond to feedback, and stay present in the communities where your buyers hang out.
